命题逻辑

Posted on 2020-02-07 Edited on 2020-04-27 In Math , Discrete Mathematics

Introduction

逻辑规则赋予了数学陈述的精确的意义，这些规则用来区分有效陈述和无效的数学陈述。除此之外，逻辑在计算机科学上也有非常多的应用，比如验证程序的正确性、电路设计、计算机程序的构建等等。

Propositional Logic

我们的讨论先从逻辑的基本组成部分的介绍讲起。

定义1 \quad 一个命题(proposition)是一个或真或假的陈述性句子(即陈述事实的语句)，但是不能既真既假。

比如下面的陈述性句子都是命题:

  1. 华盛顿是美国的首都
  2. 多伦多是加拿大的首都
  3. 1+1=31 + 1 = 31+1=3
  4. 2+2=32 + 2 = 32+2=3

其中命题1和命题3为真，命题2和命题4为假。

值得注意的是，对于“除地球外的星球有生物”，这句话是陈述句，而且其真假也是唯一的，所以它属于命题，只不过我们现在还无法判断而已。（真值确定，但未知）

下面的句子都不是命题:

  1. 几点了？
  2. 仔细阅读这个!
  3. x+1=2x + 1 = 2x+1=2
  4. x+y=zx + y = zx+y=z

句子1和2都不是命题，因为他们都不是陈述性语句。句子3和4不是命题，是因为它们既不为真，也不为假。

我们一般使用字母去表示命题变量，传统上使用的字母比如p,q,r,s,⋯p,q,r,s,\cdotsp,q,r,s,⋯。

Logical operators

The negation of a proposition

定义2 \quad 一个命题的真值(truth value)为真，如果它是一个真命题，用TTT表示； 如果一个命题为假，则这个命题的真值为假，用FFF表示。

定义3 \quad 通过已知的命题去构造新的命题，这个命题叫做复合命题。

定义4 \quad 假设ppp是一个命题，那么命题ppp的否定为：“It is not the case that p.”，用¬p¬p¬p（或者用pˉ\bar{p}pˉ​)来表示，读作“非(not)ppp“，它的真值和ppp的真值相反。

例如，"Michael’s PC runs Linux"的否定为: "It is not the case that Michael’s PC runs Linux.“即"Michael’s PC doesn’t run Linux.”

又例如，”Vandana’s smartphone has at least 32 GB of memory"的否定句为"It is not the case that Vandana’s smartphone has at least 32 GB of memory."，即"Vandana’s smartphone has less than 32GB of memory."

The conjunction of two propositions

两个命题的合取(conjunction)定义如下:

定义5 \quad Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The conjunction of ppp and qqq, denoted by p∧qp \wedge qp∧q, is the proposition “p and q”. The conjunction p∧qp \wedge qp∧q is true when ppp and qqq are both true and is false otherwise.

值得注意的是，在逻辑上，"但是(but)“这个词有时候代替"和(and)”。比如：“今天太阳很大，但是下雨了”，这句话实际上就是用了另外一种方式表达”今天太阳很大，而且下雨了“。在自然语言上，这里”但是“和”和“有着微妙的区别，但是在离散数学上，我们不考虑它们的这种微妙区别。

例如：假设

命题ppp为: “Rebecca’s PC has more than 16GB free hard disk space.”

命题qqq为: “The processor in Rebecca’s PC runs faster than 1GHz.”

那么它们的合取 p∧qp \wedge qp∧q 为: “Rebecca’s PC has more than 16GB free hard disk space, and the processor in Rebecca’s PC runs faster than 1GHz.” 或者用更加简洁的方式表达为: “Rebecca’s PC has more than 16GB free hard disk space, and its proccessor runs faster than 1GHz.”。

The disjunction of two propositions

定义6 \quad Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The disjunction of ppp and qqq, denoted by p∨qp \vee qp∨q , is the proposition “p or q”. The disjunction p∨qp \vee qp∨q is false when p and q are both false and is true otherwise.

注意，这里的or析取词对应英语中or的两种用法的其中一种——包含或(inclusive or)。还有一种or是排斥或(exclusive or)。下面的三个例子来体会一下or在英语表达中到底是哪一个or？

例如:

句子1: “Students who have taken calculus or computer science can take this class.”

句子2: “Students who have taken calculus or computer science, but not both, can enroll in this class.”

句子3: “Soup or salad comes with entree?(in a restaurant)”

其中句子1的or是inclusive or。句子2、3都是exclusive or。

The proposition is true when p is true and q is false, and when p is false and q is true. It is false when both p and q are false and when both are true.

Conditional Statements

定义8: \quad Let ppp and qqq be propositions. The conditional statement p→qp \to qp→q is the proposition “if p, then q”. The conditional statement p→qp \to qp→q is false when ppp is true and qqq is false, and true otherwise.

在这个定义中，ppp叫做hypothesis、antecedent、premise, qqq 叫做conclusion or consequence.

A conditional statement is also called an implication.所以→\to→也被叫做蕴含联结词。

下面给出conditional statement p→qp \to qp→q的其他形式的表达:

  1. if p, then q
  2. if p, q
  3. p is sufficient for q
  4. q if p
  5. q when p
  6. a neccesary condition for p is q
  7. q unless ¬p¬p¬p
  8. p imlies q
  9. p only if q
  10. a sufficient condition for q is p
  11. q whenever p
  12. q is a neccesary for p
  13. q follows from p

比如p表示"Maria learns discrete mathematics",q表示"Maria will find a good job."，那么p→qp \to qp→q的英语表达为:

  1. If Maria learns discrete mathematics, then she will find a good job.
  2. Maria will find a good job when she learns discrete mathematics.
  3. Maria will find a good job unless she doesn’t learn discrete mathematics.

等等…

Converse, Contropositive and Inverse

我们可以通过条件语句p→qp \to qp→q得到新的条件语句。特别地，这里介绍三种相关的条件语句，它们有特殊的名字。

The proposition q→pq \to pq→p is called the converse of p→qp \to qp→q.

The proposition ¬q→¬p\neg q \to \neg p¬q→¬p is called the contropositive of p→qp \to qp→q.

The proposition ¬p→¬q\neg p \to \neg q¬p→¬q is called the inverse of p→qp \to qp→q.

这三个条件语句都是从p→qp \to qp→q形成的，只有contropositive总是和p→qp \to qp→q有相同的真值。

定义9 \quad 两个命题等价当且仅当它们总是有相同的真值。

Therefore, a conditional statement is equivalent to its contropositve. The converse and the inverse of a conditional statement are also equivalent.

Biconditional Statement

Biconditional statement 是另一种表达两个命题有相同的真值的方式。

定义10 \quad Let p and q be propositions. The biconditional statement p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q is the proposition “p if and only if q”. The biconditional statement p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q is true when p and q have the same truth values, and is false otherwise. Biconditional statements are also called bi-implications.

There are some other common ways to express p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q:

  • p is neccessary and sufficient for q
  • if p then q, and conversely
  • p iff q

例子： 假设

p: “You can take the flight.”

q: “You buy a ticket.”

Then p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q is the proposition “You can take the flight if and only if you buy a ticket.”

Propositional Equivalences

Classification of Compound Proposition

定义11 \quad A compound proposition that is always true, no matter what the truth values of the propositional variables that occur in it, is called a tautology. A compound proposition that is always false is called a contradiction. A compound proposition that is neither a tautology nor a contradiction is called a contingency.

例如，p∨¬pp \vee \neg pp∨¬p是tautology, p∧¬pp \wedge \neg pp∧¬p是contradiction.

Logical Equivalence

定义12 \quad The compound proposition ppp and q are called logically equivalent if p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q is a tautology, denoted by p≡qp \equiv qp≡q.

注意: ≡\equiv≡ 不是一个逻辑联结符号，所以p≡qp \equiv qp≡q不是一个复合命题，而是陈述了p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q是一个重言式。另外，有时候用⇔\Leftrightarrow⇔来替代≡\equiv≡。

根据逻辑等价的定义，可以知道证明逻辑等价的方法之一就是用真值表，若命题ppp和qqq的真值表的每一行都一样，那么p↔qp \leftrightarrow qp↔q是一个重言式，则说明p≡qp \equiv qp≡q.

Propositional Satisfiability

定义13 \quad A compound proposition is satisfiable if there is an assignment of truth values to its variables that makes it true. When no such assignments exists, that is, when the compound proposition is false for all assignments of truth values to its variables, the compound proposition is unsatisfiable. When we find a particular assignment of truth values that makes a compound proposition true, we have shown that it is satisfiable; such an assignment is called a solution of this particular satisfiability problem.
